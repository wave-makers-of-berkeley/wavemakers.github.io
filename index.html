What we do

Our mission, vision, and commitments.

Community

We strive to connect the CAP scholars at UC Berkeley with unique engagement and programming to bridge the gap between the CAP program and the campus.

Mentorship

We provide a platform to match upper-class mentors to new student mentees, aiming to provide a smoother transition to college for new scholars.

Leadership

We welcome members of the CAP community to grow and develop with our teams, and we aim to provide a platform for creativity and innovation.

Our Story

See where we've started and where we are now.

  • Spring 2018:

    Planning Initiative

    Through a call for action by our college coach, Berkeley Wave Makers came together and began the planning initiative for a “campus meet-up group.” Over 20 CAP students attended the initial planning meeting and discussed the structure and governance for what would later be known as the “Wave Makers of Berkeley.”

  • Summer 2018:

    First General Meeting & Inaugural Executive Board

    Our first general meeting was held on campus & live-streamed online. Members in attendance approved the Constitution for our student group and voted in our inaugural executive board. Many members of this inaugural executive remained with us today serving the Wave Maker community on campus.

  • Fall 2018:

    First Campus Meetup

    Our first campus meetup was held at Victory Point Cafe where all CAP students who could make it came to meet other CAP students at UC Berkeley. This was a chance for CAP students to build community and learn more about the Wave Maker of Berkeley. Over 30 Berkeley students joined us and bonded with other CAP students with pizza and board games.

  • Spring 2019:

    Independent Branding

    We were able to solidify a brand that symbolizes our mission while reflecting a design that appeals to our audience, CAP students of UC Berkeley, with the help of Innovative Design at Berkeley. This has helped us recruit more students and become more noticeable as a student organization rather than a scholarship program.

  • Summer 2019:

    Welcoming BA-4 - Summer Bridge Extension

    We hosted the first-ever student-led extension program for CAP Summer Bridge, the first of its kind for any Wave Maker college campuses. Ten volunteers of current Berkeley Wave Makers came during summer break to introduce our campus to our latest addition, the BA-4 cohort.

  • Fall 2019:

    Team Expansion - Project Committees

    After a full academic year on campus, the Executive Board has decided to expand service & leadership roles by adding three core project committees--Education, Programming, and Publicity & Outreach--welcoming 12 new team members on this journey of community engagement.

  • Spring 2020:

    Wave Maker Newsletter

    We developed a monthly newsletter to help CAP students be up to date with any resources or events that can professionally or personally develop students. After many iterations, we were able to find a structure that can present important announcements for CAP students, resources and highlight CAP students and their educational journey.

  • Summer 2020:

    Welcoming BA-5 During COVID-19 - Vitual Engagements

    Due to COVID-19 we were not able to host our yearly Wave Makers of Berkeley summer bridge. We then adjusted our resources to engage virtually which is where we created a three part video that introduces WMB and gives educationally and personal tips and resources on how to successfully navigate the university.

Mentorship & Development

The mentorship and development committee strives to create a sense of community among wave-makers through our mentorship program. The mentorship program pairs mentees with mentors who share similar interests/majors to provide mentees with the opportunity to ask mentors any question they might have about academics or interests outside of academics. Aside from our mentorship program, our committee hopes to provide various opportunities throughout the semesters that foster professional development in our community. Some of these opportunities would be hosting Q&A sessions to provide tips on classes or clubs, nights where we fix our resumes or events in partnership with the career center.

Programming

The Programming Team is dedicated to plan social and academic events with the goal of building a strong community that will allow Wave Makers to not feel alone in their journey at Cal. In this community, we want students across all waves to be able to share their experiences, find someone who has taken similar classes as them, or find someone that is in the same major. This year, we want to have Fall and Spring Meet Ups, and an academic event each semester. We have a lot of ideas that range from study jams to art nights.

Publicity & Outreach

With over 90 Wave-Makers on campus, the Publicity Team is dedicated to keeping us all updated on the WMB life on campus! Our main avenue of connecting with students on campus is through the weekly Wave Maker Newsletter, where we announce upcoming events, important WMB updates and highlight different Wave Makers of Berkeley. We believe it’s important to recognize people in our small community especially when we are at such a big school! Our team hopes to keep us all (virtually) connected!

Philanthropy & Service

The goal of the Philanthropy & Service team is to seek opportunities to engage wave makers in service projects whether it be through our own initiatives or by collaborating with other organizations on campus. This will allow us to make a positive impact not only on our wave maker community, but also the Berkeley community as a whole. Additionally, we seek to engage with community partners to expand and grow our organization financially. This fundraising will provide the funds we need to maintain our projects in order to continue to expand our resources and continue to support our wave makers. We are so excited to see our project ideas come to fruition and hope to continue to foster the safe space we have created!

Culture

The Culture Committee will work to build up our organizational culture by creating socials and programs for our team members. Some potential activities include multicultural potlocks and methods of displaying members’ talents such as a digital art gallery. Since WMB has multiple committees and members of varying involvement, the culture committee will bridge our teams with the broader WMB community. Through their programming, the culture committee will prioritize disrupting the status quo at Cal by helping wavemakers support each other in navigating the large institution. This will be an internal team, which means members will need to be part of a core committee to join.

Finance & Development

Finance & Development is responsible for the distribution of funding for Wave Maker activities on and off campus. We take on the logistics and financial planning when it comes to our programming and events such as allocating funds for food and Wave Maker merchandise. Overall, we collaborate with every different team that makes up Wave-Makers at Berkeley and are excited to improve and expand upon our programming this year.
